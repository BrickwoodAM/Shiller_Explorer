What CAPE Yield Tells Us

Since 1881, we know the CAPE yield each month and the real total return over the following 10 years. Higher yields (cheaper markets) have historically led to better future returns. Hover over the timeline to explore what happened from any starting month.

Pick a Starting Valuation

Use the sliders to choose any starting CAPE yield — today's, or any other. Every month in history that began near that level stays highlighted while the rest fade, showing the full spread of what the next 10 years actually delivered from that starting point.

How Confident Can We Be?

This chart keeps the starting valuation you chose above and varies how long you hold. For each month in history that began at that valuation, it shows the annualised returns investors earned over different holding periods. Over short holding periods the outcomes are scattered widely; the longer the holding period, the more tightly they cluster. Time helps convert uncertainty into probability.

Why CAPE Yield Works

Total return has two parts: the fundamental return the businesses deliver (income plus earnings growth), and the re-rating or de-rating investors apply to it. High valuations usually occur when the outlook is favourable. Yet history shows almost no relationship between the valuation you start at and the fundamentals that follow — the teal dots lie nearly flat. What starting valuation does predict is the rating change: expensive markets tend to be followed by de-rating, and cheap markets by re-rating. That — not better fundamentals — is why starting yield matters.
